Understand the paper before the paper.

PaperTrail is built to turn an intimidating academic paper into a complete pre-reading map: the problem, theory, notation, method, evidence, limitations, and future work, so the original paper feels familiar before you open it.

The goal is research-grade paper intelligence.

A strong PaperTrail guide should make a researcher feel as if the paper has already been reconstructed: argument, theory, method, notation, evidence, limitations, and research opportunities. When they read the original paper afterward, the structure and critique points should already feel familiar.

Reconstruct the argument

Identify the research question, gap, core claim, and the logic connecting them.

Teach the background

Prepare the theory, assumptions, concepts, and field vocabulary needed to read well.

Decode the technical layer

Translate notation, equations, methods, figures, and evidence into plain language.

Build scholarly judgment

Surface limitations, open questions, future research directions, and critique angles.
